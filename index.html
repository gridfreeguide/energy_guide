Why More Homeowners Are Preparing for Power Outages in 2026

In recent years, power outages have become more frequent and unpredictable.

From overloaded grids to extreme weather, more households are starting to realize how dependent they are on a single energy source.

When the power goes out, most homes are not prepared.

No refrigeration. No internet. No way to charge devices — and in some cases, even water systems can stop working.

Because of this, many homeowners are starting to look for ways to reduce their reliance on the grid.

Some invest in generators. Others consider solar systems.

But these options can be expensive, require maintenance, or depend on fuel availability.

Recently, alternative approaches have started gaining attention — especially methods that don’t rely entirely on traditional systems.
