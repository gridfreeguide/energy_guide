Why More Homeowners Are Preparing for Power Outages in 2026

In recent years, power outages have become more frequent and unpredictable. From overloaded grids to extreme weather events, more households are realizing how dependent they are on a single energy source.

When the power goes out, most homes are not prepared. No refrigeration. No internet. No way to charge devices — and in some cases, even water systems can stop working.

Some homeowners invest in generators or solar systems, but these options can be expensive, require maintenance, or depend on fuel availability.

Recently, alternative approaches have been gaining attention — especially methods that reduce reliance on traditional power systems without complicated installation.
